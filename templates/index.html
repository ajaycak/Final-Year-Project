Importance of Crop Yield

Crop cultivation is essential worldwide, providing food, livelihoods, and economic stability. It sustains populations, supports rural communities, and drives global trade. In nations like India, agriculture is crucial for livelihoods, yet it contributes only 14% to the GDP. Leveraging data and analytics can optimize farming, increasing efficiency and profitability. Thus, it is of due importance to get an estimate of the cultivation before employing all the resources to the task.

Optimizing Yield Prediction

To optimize crop yield prediction, we can integrate advanced technologies like machine learning and deep learning for precise data collection and analysis. Incorporate historical data, weather forecasts, and soil information for accurate modeling and continuous refinement through feedback loops enhances predictions, enabling proactive farming strategies and sustainable agricultural practices.

Benefits of better yield

By accurately estimating yields, farmers can optimize planting decisions, manage resources effectively, and mitigate risks associated with fluctuating market demands and environmental conditions. Yield predictions enable policymakers, researchers, and stakeholders to make informed decisions, fostering sustainable agricultural practices and enhancing global food production efficiency.

About the project

This project suggests using deep learning and machine learning techniques to forecast crop yield throughout India and the world, where nearly half of the population depends on agriculture for a living. The study trains the models using a variety of input variables, including weather information, soil characteristics and crop character traits. The findings demonstrate that models based on machine learning and deep learning are effective at accurately predicting crop yield. Intelligent choices about crop production and management can be made using the proposed approach by farmers and policymakers. Using stacked ensemble models of Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, Bagging regressor and RNN as the meta-learner layer, the project evaluates crop yield.
